Dota 2

Dota 2 is a free-to-play multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed and published by Valve Corporation. It is one of the most popular esports games globally, known for its deep strategy, intense team-based gameplay, and complex mechanics.

CS:GO

CS (Counter-Strike: Global Offensive) is a highly popular first-person shooter (FPS) game developed by Valve and Hidden Path Entertainment. Released in 2012, it's the fourth game in the Counter-Strike series and remains one of the most played and successful multiplayer games in the world.

Valorant

Valorant is a free-to-play first-person tactical shooter developed by Riot Games, released in June 2020. The game combines precise shooting mechanics with strategic team-based gameplay, making it one of the most popular esports titles today.

Fortnite

Fortnite is an immensely popular online video game developed by Epic Games and released in 2017. It has become a cultural phenomenon, attracting millions of players worldwide with its vibrant graphics and innovative gameplay.

League of Legends

League of Legends (often abbreviated as LoL) is a highly popular multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Riot Games. Launched in 2009, it has become one of the most played and influential games in the world, particularly in the esports scene. In a typical match, two teams of five players compete on a map called Summoner's Rift.
